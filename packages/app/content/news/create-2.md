# Create 2.0 is Finally Here!

Well, folks, it's happening. Today marks the release of Create 2.0! Many of us have eagerly awaited this release for literal years, hope waning as time continued to march on inexorably with no signs of a real update.

![It's happening!](https://media.giphy.com/media/v1.Y2lkPTc5MGI3NjExdGh4cTJ5MjJ0NGk3bmNjMzNzYm4xa2Jvc2toaTYzODFzNGVkdzV1NyZlcD12MV9naWZzX3NlYXJjaCZjdD1n/MZocLC5dJprPTcrm65/giphy.gif)

And now our wait is over. Because it's here. It's here! It's fiiiiinalllllyy here!!

## What's New? The Era

Not to be melodramatic or hyperbolic (okay, maybe a bit of both of those), but I think Create 2.0 will help usher in a new kind of publisher tooling.

I mean, yes: it brings long-awaited parity features like **adjustable servings**, **unit conversion**, **checklists**, and **review replies**.

Yes, it introduces a brand-new, never-before-seen, state-of-the-art, cutting-edge, currently-exclusive-but-soon-to-be-copied **Interactive Mode** experience to your recipes.

Yes, it brings performance improvements like a reduced plugin footprint (only 2.8MB zipped! because you care about that) and even-more-optimized JavaScript and CSS bundles (like itty-bitty so you can keep that coveted Lighthouse score), countless bug fixes, security improvements, and enhancements.

Yes, it introduces a bit more fun to your day with achievements, micro-animations, greetings, and... confetti.

![confetti](/img/news/create-2/confetti.webp)

Yes, it brings **keyboard shortcuts**, searchable and **illustrative settings** (seriously. try it! configuring settings has never been so fun), and more streamlined workflows to simplify your experience.
![settings](/img/news/create-2/settings.webp)

- Say "au revoir!" to 5-step List item addition and "bonjour!" to bulk-adding list items.
- Say "sayonara!" to manually sifting through cards to find what you're looking for and "ohayou!" to filters, sorting, and useful information on card hover in collections.

And yes, it introduces a beautiful, built-in importing experience to bring your recipes and how-to cards home to Create (enable the importer in Create's settings).

![importer](/img/news/create-2/importer.webp)

And yes, there's a new **Featured Review** block to highlight your readers' praises. And **new card themes** (and more on the way). And you can add **custom CSS** to your cards directly in Create's settings. And yes, you can add **custom HTML** between list items for your ad provider (ANY ad provider) to target.

Yes, yes. There are all of those things and more.

## Waxing Poetic

But in my opinion, the real value is not found in a feature or enhancement or fix (though they're great, too), it's in a new way of looking at the tools you use every day.

Because here's the sad truth: most products suck. Even if they work–even if they work really well!–they're ugly and boring and miserable to work with. They look and feel like the software your accountant used in 1996.

There's been precious little motivation–especially in the WordPress world–to do anything different. Just do the easy thing, make this plugin like every other plugin, but a little bit different, or more customizable, or... whatever.

## Build Better Products

When Create was first launched in 2018, we had the same goal as I now maintain: **make better products**. Not just better than the other recipe card plugins, better than any of the other plugins, better than your bank, your host, your dog groomer's scheduling, your go-to hotel booking site, your newsletter management app. (*Psst.* here's a secret code for 100 thorough readers: thanksforreading) And not just incrementally better, but fundamentally different.

In many ways, we succeeded. And at its height of popularity, nearly 15,000 creators like yourself used and loved Create, with the most common praise being that it was "great experience" or the like.

Now that Create is back and better-than-ever, that vision is still strong.

::callout{type="quote"}
My hope is that you'll look at the update and say, "wow–this is so fun to use!" (and if you don't say that, please let me know how I could make it so!)
::
